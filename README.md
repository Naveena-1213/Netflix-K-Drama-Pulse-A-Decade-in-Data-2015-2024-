# Netflix-K-Drama-Pulse-A-Decade-in-Data-2015-2024

## Background and Overview
The global rise of K-Dramas has been phenomenal over the past decade, with Netflix playing a major role in taking these series beyond South Korea to audiences worldwide.

This project, "K-Drama Pulse: A Decade in Data (2015‚Äì2024)", analyzes the trends, performance, and audience reception of K-Dramas on Netflix from 2015 to 2024. By leveraging data extracted from TMDB using Python, we explore how the number of dramas, genres, actors, and ratings have evolved over time.

## Objective

The main goal is to provide actionable insights for content strategists, producers, and marketers, helping them understand viewer preferences and make data-driven decisions for future programming.

## Data Structure Overview
The dataset was systematically extracted from TMDB using Python API calls, followed by rigorous preprocessing to ensure accuracy and consistency using Excel 

Key variables:

*`Name`: Title of the drama

*`Original Network`: Source network

*`Year of Release`: Release year

*`Number of Episodes`: Total episodes

*`Duration`: Average episode duration (minutes)

*`Rating`: Audience/critic rating (scale of 1‚Äì10)

*`Genre`: Primary and secondary genre tags

*`Synopsis`: Series plot summary

*`Actor Name`: Main cast

The dataset also includes a dedicated issue log detailing null value treatment, duplicate handling, and transformation logic ‚Äî ensuring full data governance and transparency.

## Insights Deep Dive
### Yearly Performance
* Strong correlation between the number of series released and rising audience ratings indicates that higher volume has not compromised quality.

* Consistent popularity of longer formats (average 24 episodes) suggests that audiences enjoy immersive, character-driven storytelling.

## Recommendations
Based on the findings, here are strategic recommendations:

* Continue Investing in Drama & Hybrid Genres: Maintain focus on Drama but explore further hybridization with genres like Sci-Fi, Thriller, and Historical to capture new audience segments.

* Support New Talent: While leveraging established actors, consider introducing fresh faces to diversify offerings and tap into new fandoms.

* Focus on Quality Over Quantity: Despite the rise in series count, sustaining or improving the average rating is crucial for global competitiveness.

* Expand International Marketing: The consistent high ratings signal strong content ‚Äî targeted marketing can further boost reach, especially in non-Asian markets.

* Explore Shorter Formats: Consider experimenting with mini-series or short-format content to attract viewers who prefer quick binge-worthy shows.
